AUSTRALIAN AMBULANCE SERVICES

Response Times by State and Territory

Response time is the time in minutes from when an emergency call is recieved until the paramedics arrive at the scene.

This map shows state-wide average response times. In the last ten years, the lowest average response time is 12.5 minutes for the Australian Capital Territory (2015), while the highest is 33.4 minutes in Tasmania (2021).

The Australian Capital Territory has the best response times in Australia, which may be because it is such a small geographical area. However, Western Australia is also a strong performer and it is the largest state. Tasmania has consistently performed the worst across the country.

Of interest is the effect of the COVID pandemic, which can be observed by increased response times across the board.

How does State Ambulance Service Spending Vary?

Ambulance spending per person varies signficantly between states.

Western Australia's spending has remained relatively constant over the last ten years, as have its response times (which can be seen in the above map).

Tasmania started to increase ambulance spending in 2018 after a 2017 increase in response times.

How Frequently are Ambulance Targets Met in Victorian LGAs?

Victorian Ambulance Response Target Rate 2019

Ambulance Victoria has a target of responding to Code 1 incidents within 15 minutes at least 90% of the time in urban areas and 85% state wide. Code 1 incidents are those where patients require urgent paramedic assistance.

Inner metropolitan areas and areas near large cities are more likely to meet these targets. Rural areas often fall well below, for instance Loddon Shire only meets its target 31.1% of the time.

Cardiac Arrest Survival Rates

People suffering cardiac arrests that are witnessed by paramedics have a better survival rate than those who are not not witnessed. New South Wales, Northern Territory and Western Australia all have rates above the Australian average of 49.8%.
Where a cardiac arrest is not witness by a paramedic the average Australian survival rate drops to 23.3%. The Australian Capital Territory, Queensland and Victoria all have survival rates above the average.
